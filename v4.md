## 4.4 Clinical Content Standardization (Weeks 2-6)

Mandatory card template:
1. Overview
2. Classification
3. Symptoms
4. Red Flags
5. Diagnostics (algorithm)
6. Differential diagnosis (table)
7. Laboratory norms (table + threshold notes)
8. Treatment (lines + escalation)
9. Follow-up
10. Prognosis
11. Clinical cases
12. Sources / guideline versions

Formatting standards:
- Differential diagnosis in comparison tables (`feature`, `supports`, `against`).
- Lab norms in structured tables (`parameter`, `normal range`, `action threshold`).
- Diagnostics in step flow blocks (`if -> then -> next`).
- Treatment with recommendation strength where available.

## 12) Guideline Source Governance

Primary source portals:
- EAU Guidelines: `https://uroweb.org/guidelines`
- AUA Guidelines & Quality: `https://www.auanet.org/guidelines-and-quality`
- CUA Guidelines: `https://www.cua.org/guidelines`
- Russian clinical recommendation rubricator: `https://cr.minzdrav.gov.ru/`
- USANZ professional portal: `https://www.usanz.org.au/`

Important governance note:
- AUA guideline pages include explicit reuse restrictions for third-party AI systems. UroMed must store AUA as a source target and citation pointer, but detailed recommendation transfer should be handled through licensed access or human clinical editorial review.
- CUA includes member-gated materials. Public metadata can guide source tracking, but locked documents require authorized clinical review.
- Russian recommendations must be verified against the official rubricator before adding or changing treatment statements.

Clinical content rule:
- No new treatment, diagnostic threshold, drug dose, or follow-up interval should be added without a `sourcePack` entry containing source body, document title, year/version, URL, and review date.
